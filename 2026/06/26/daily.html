🥇 Linux Foundation and 20 tech giants launch Akrites to fix open-source flaws before AI-powered attacks hit

Linux Foundation and 20 major tech companies formed Akrites — a coalition to identify and patch vulnerabilities in open-source software before attackers exploit them with AI-driven tools.

Why it matters: AI-powered exploits are accelerating; fixing flaws at scale requires industry-wide coordination, not just reactive patches.

White House reins in OpenAI’s GPT-5.6

The White House is now requiring case-by-case approval for OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 deployments — a shift from broad rollouts to targeted oversight, with agencies reviewing each customer’s use case.

Why it matters: This sets a precedent for granular control over advanced models, not just blanket restrictions.

Anthropic Thinks Its Own Success Is Key to Making AI Safe

Anthropic argues its rapid growth is necessary for responsible AI development — countering critics who see its expanding influence as a risk.

Why it matters: The debate over whether safety requires centralization or decentralization is heating up as models advance.

OpenAI’s GPT 5.6 rollout now requires US government approval on a “customer by customer basis”

OpenAI’s GPT-5.6 deployments are now subject to US government review per customer — a move mirroring the White House’s broader push for tighter AI oversight.

Why it matters: This could slow adoption but may also reduce misuse risks for high-stakes applications.
