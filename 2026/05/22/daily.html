🥇 Google I/O showed how the path for AI-driven science is shifting

Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis called this moment the “foothills of the singularity” at Google I/O — a rare public framing of AI’s role in scientific discovery as a turning point.

Why it matters: The claim reframes AI not just as a tool but as a co-pilot for breakthroughs, raising stakes for how labs validate AI-generated hypotheses.

Finnish phone-maker HMD bundles Indian AI chatbot onto new smartphone

HMD pre-loads Sarvam’s Indus chatbot on its latest device to target India’s local market — the app supports 22 Indic languages and runs entirely on-device.

Why it matters: On-device AI for non-English markets could become the default for global OEMs, not just a niche feature.

Can OpenAI’s ‘Master of Disaster’ Fix AI’s Reputation Crisis?

OpenAI taps global affairs chief Chris Lehane to reframe AI’s societal impact — his playbook: tone down the debate and push for laws that don’t slow OpenAI’s growth.

Why it matters: Hiring a crisis PR veteran signals OpenAI sees reputation as a long-term risk, not just a short-term headache.

Waymo expands pause to four cities as robotaxis keep driving into floods

Waymo suspends service in Atlanta and San Antonio after vehicles repeatedly drove into flooded roads — adding to existing pauses in Phoenix and Austin.

Why it matters: Weather-related failures are becoming a pattern, not outliers, for autonomous fleets.

The Gulf’s AI Boom Has an Undersea Cable Problem
